The Team


We love design

Game Designers don't just design games. We have to understand the experience we want to create for players, and then design the game features or changes needed to bring this experience into reality. From designing systems to balancing League, creating new content or building never-before-seen mechanics, millions of players will interact with your work on a daily basis.



While every Rioter is a champion for the player experience—and many Rioters help us define content, rules, challenges and balance within a game—we focus exclusively on those tasks. Holistic design sensibilities, problem-solving, agile execution, and thoughtful communication are our necessary tools as we work on League and other R&D projects.

We Are Rioters


Riot Games was founded in 2006 to develop, publish, and support the most player-focused games in the world. In 2009, we released our debut title, League of Legends, to worldwide acclaim. League has gone on to be the most-played PC game in the world and a key driver of the explosive growth of esports.


As League enters its second decade, we’re continuing to evolve the game while delivering new experiences with games including Legends of Runeterra, League of Legends: Wild Rift, and multiple work-in-progress titles; bringing Teamfight Tactics to mobile platforms; and exploring the world of Runeterra through multimedia projects across TV, music, comic books, and more.


The annual League of Legends World Championship features qualified esports teams from 13 international leagues. Worlds is the most widely viewed and followed esport tournament, and it is among the largest and most popular gaming and sporting events in the world.


Founded by Brandon Beck and Marc Merrill, we are headquartered in Los Angeles, California, and have 2,500+ Rioters in 20+ offices worldwide. We’ve been featured on numerous lists including Fortune’s “100 Best Companies to Work For,” “25 Best Companies to Work in Technology,” “100 Best Workplaces for Millennials,” and “50 Best Workplaces for Flexibility.
